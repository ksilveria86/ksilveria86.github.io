Kati Silveria

Visual Storyteller + Printmaker

About the Artist

Kati Silveria is a visual artist and designer based in Spokane, Washington. Kati was raised mostly in the Inland Northwest.

In 2021, she received BAs in Visual Communication Design and Studio Art (Printmaking) from Eastern Washington University. She has worked with the Kalispel Tribe on an ELLO project and did design work for the Pink Ladies Tournament in Redding, California. She was exhibited in Eastern Washington University’s Creative Symposium in the Spring of 2020, for her hand-built relief comic book.

During her time at Eastern, Kati was influenced by the Printmaking Professor, Margot Casstevens, and Design Professor, Simeon Mills, for visual storytelling through comics utilizing the printmaking processes.

General Artist Statement

I am a dirty artist; let me explain. I work in the mediums of printmaking, drawing, painting, mixed media, digital, and clay. If the digital medium were physically tangible at all, I’d be sure to be covered in its mess. I am a visual storyteller and artist who creates creative narratives through my work. It is in my nature to tell stories within my work, which is why I have such a tactile approach to the creation of my art, and end up getting so dirty in the process. Utilizing different art mediums, such as printmaking, drawing, painting, and digital, I developed my narrative stories, characters, and worlds for enjoyment. Applying the techniques of printmaking such as Relief, Collagraph, Lithography, Mono-point and Dry-point; I create the characters and scenery through line work, copic markers, and rice paper (chine colle). Using mixed media and digital for texture visuals in the stories creates a unique way of viewing a different world. I hand-write the story to give an organic feeling while reading the story, because most stories now are created in a printed type.

Through the process of creating stories in these mediums, I learned that I could take these concepts to another level by digitizing the stories into merchandise designs. I have always been a comical person that relishes in stories that make people laugh. I wanted to develop my skills as a visual storyteller further by creating designs that others will enjoy and want to wear.

The concept of taking a comic strip and turning it into a t-shirt design takes my passion for storytelling and the idea of owning my own business into one cohesive structure for me to navigate through as an artist. Creating a business out of a passion is an internal goal that drives me to create more humorous stories that can be used for others entertainment. I have always been a believer that art mediums can be intertwined for different purposes and usage.
